# Key holders

this page will eventually be the receipt: every large $LAWS wallet, what it does, and which ones physically can't move. address by address, verifiable on the explorer.

right now it's the shortest chapter in the handbook, because:

## nobody holds $LAWS, because $LAWS doesn't exist yet

the real token launches with mainnet (see the **roadmap**). there is no supply, no holders chart, no LP, no timelock, no top-10 concentration to squint at. anyone claiming to hold or sell $LAWS today is lying to you.

what does exist is **testnet LAWS**: a stand-in ERC-20 on robinhood chain testnet, airdropped 50 per mint so duels have something to stake. it has no value, no market, and it gets wiped whenever the testnet contracts redeploy. it's monopoly money with a ticker.

## what this page will show at mainnet

when $LAWS launches, the plan (per the roadmap, no dates) is a fixed supply with a transparent split, and this page becomes the breakdown:

- **the locked LP**: the launchpad pools the full supply at creation and locks the LP in its locker. no dev key, no withdrawal.
- **any dev buy**: made in the open at launch, same terms as everyone, wallet disclosed.
- **the keeper / ops float**: the wallet that funds ops and keeps the game running.
- **the dev wallet**: ops budget, trust-based, every move public on the explorer.
- **the players**: everything else.

exact numbers, percentages, and addresses are not locked yet. they'll be published here and on the site's trust page at launch, with links so you can verify every line yourself rather than take my word for it.

until then: there are no keys to hold. go shoot something. 🏹
