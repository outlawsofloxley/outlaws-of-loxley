# outlaws of loxley: the player's handbook

pixel-art outlaws. sherwood forest. a chain named after a stockbroker. what could go wrong.

this is the player's manual for **outlaws of loxley**. start at the top, walk through to the bottom, and by the end you'll know how to mint, fight, sell, die, get cut down from the gallows, and where you sit on the leaderboard.

## the big honest banner first

**this game is on testnet.** robinhood chain testnet, to be exact. nothing here costs real money, nothing here is worth real money, and the $LAWS you earn is play money by design. mainnet on robinhood chain is on the roadmap, not on a date. treat everything you do right now as a playtest, because that's what it is.

## what it is, in one breath

2,000 pixel-art outlaws of sherwood, plus 1 king on top: robin himself, token id 2001, golden longbow. you mint one for a micro test price, get 50 LAWS airdropped to play with, stake LAWS to duel other outlaws, and the winner takes the pot. lose three in a row and your outlaw swings at the gallows until someone pays to cut them down. 🏹

every outlaw is rolled fresh on mint: on-chain name, six stats, a weapon from the sherwood catalogue, deterministic pixel art. same token id, same outlaw, forever.

## who this is for

- you've used a wallet before, even just once
- you can find your way around a basic dapp (connect, sign, confirm)
- you're happy poking at a testnet game before it's "real"

if you've never touched a wallet, the **getting started** chapter walks you through it. testnet is actually the perfect place to learn: mistakes are free here.

## what this isn't

- not a token sale. there's no token to sell. $LAWS launches at mainnet, not before.
- not a finished product. it's a small solo build on a young chain, being played in public.
- not financial anything. it's a game about arrows and bad decisions.

i'm a solo dev. that's the charm. it's also the limit. i'd rather you understood the trade-offs than felt sold to.
