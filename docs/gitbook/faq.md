# FAQ

questions people actually ask, plus the ones i'd ask if i found this project cold.

## is this real money?

no. the game is live on **robinhood chain testnet** only. mint prices are micro amounts of free faucet eth, the LAWS you stake was airdropped free with your mint, and none of it can be sold for anything. mainnet, where money becomes real, is a roadmap phase, not a date.

## so what's $LAWS then?

the game token, short for out-LAWS. the real one launches with mainnet: fixed supply, LP seeded and burned, the works (see **trust signals**). what you're holding today is testnet LAWS, play money for duels. 50 per mint.

## when mainnet?

not locked yet. the honest order of operations is on the **roadmap**: handles and domain first, then the mainnet deploy on robinhood chain. no date will appear in this handbook until it's real.

## will my testnet outlaws carry over to mainnet?

assume not. testnet contracts get redeployed as the game changes, and mainnet will be a fresh mint. testnet is the playtest.

## what chain is this exactly?

robinhood chain testnet, chain id **46630**. rpc at `https://rpc.testnet.chain.robinhood.com`, explorer at [explorer.testnet.chain.robinhood.com](https://explorer.testnet.chain.robinhood.com). the connect flow on the site adds it to your wallet for you.

## where do i get testnet eth?

the faucet: https://faucet.testnet.chain.robinhood.com. it has a browser bot-check. free eth, enough for plenty of mints and gallows rescues.

## where are the contract addresses?

on the site, not in this handbook. testnet contracts get torn down and redeployed, so any address printed here would eventually be a lie. the current set lives on the site's trust/links page at [outlaws-of-loxley.vercel.app](https://outlaws-of-loxley.vercel.app). never trust a loxley address from anywhere else.

## where's the discord / telegram / x?

coming soon. no official socials exist yet, which means **any** account claiming to be outlaws of loxley right now isn't us. when handles are claimed they'll be listed on the site and in the **links** chapter.

## what happens if i lose three duels in a row?

your outlaw hangs at the gallows. they can't fight until someone (you, or literally anyone) pays the cut-down cost, which scales with rarity and win record. full rules in **the gallows** chapter. 💀

## can i sell or transfer my outlaws?

yes. peer-to-peer marketplace on the site (priced in testnet eth), or a normal ERC-721 transfer from the outlaw's page. rating, record, and dead-or-alive state travel with the nft. listed outlaws can't duel until you cancel the listing.

## how are names like "Bandit of Blyth" made?

rolled on-chain at mint from pools of medieval first names and bynames baked into the contract. deterministic from the token id, immutable forever. no renaming.

## why does the art look like that?

on purpose. every outlaw is a deterministic 32×44 pixel archer rendered from the token id: hoods, cloaks, drawn bows, forest backgrounds that get fancier with rarity. no servers to die, no images to lose. same input, same outlaw, every time.

## who's the king?

robin of loxley, token id 2001. one of one, all stats maxed at 18, starting rating 2,000, and the only golden longbow in existence. dev-minted, never part of the public 2,000. if you meet him, aim well.

## is this safe? is it audited?

testnet, so the stakes are currently zero by construction. the contracts carry hard caps and on-chain commitments (see **trust signals**), and the test suite passes clean, but there's no paid audit and it's a solo build. when real money enters at mainnet, read the trust page again before you spend a cent.
